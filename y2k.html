Jabberwacky & Cleverbot

For many years, there was very little actual development in artificial intelligence chatbots, with the vast majority of them being extremely similar to ELIZA.

However, this finally changed in 1997, when programmer Rollo Carpenter created the Jabberwacky chatbot. Unlike ELIZA, which kept a stern, therapist tone, Jabberwacky was intended for casual conversation - a peer that one could converse with as if they were a friend.

Jabberwacky was presented on an internet page, and initially had very little conversational skills beyond common pleasantries. However, Jabberwacky learned through human interaction, and took on the idea of an ever-growing intelligence that gets closer and closer to sentience as it learns from the conversations it has with humans.

Eventually, Jabberwacky transformed into Cleverbot, a website that is still up to this day, and receives thousands of users per day. While Jabberwacky/Cleverbot is now considered obsolete by current technological standards, it is still a revolutionary concept that has data still being used to train artificial intelligence models today.
