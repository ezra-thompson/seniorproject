The Story of ELIZA

The first real example of artificial intelligence was ELIZA, an artificial intelligence developed at MIT by artificial intelligence visionary Joseph Weizenbaum in 1967. ELIZA’s main purpose was to provide therapy to users - the model used the Rogerian psychotherapy theory, which purported that traumas can be cured by repeating the patient’s words back to them and making them reflect on their thoughts.

Despite being developed in the 1960’s, ELIZA had unprecedented staying power, and was an extremely popular software up until the late 1990’s. While the artificial intelligence was extremely barebones and rarely able to truly understand the inquiry at hand, ELIZA had successfully proven one thing - humanity was willing to accept artificial intelligence as their digital peers.
